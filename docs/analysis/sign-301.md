# Sign *301: The Key to the Lock

## The Most Important Unknown Sign in Linear A

Sign *301 appears on **48 documents** — more frequently than the sign for grain. It has no equivalent in Linear B, meaning it represents a sound that Greek doesn't have. It sits at the exact center of the libation formula, blocking our ability to read the opening phrase. Solve *301, and the oldest prayer in Europe opens up.

### As Standalone Logogram (36 attestations)

**32 nodules at Haghia Triada** — *301 is the ONLY sign stamped on the seal. Whatever commodity or authorization it marks, it was important enough to stand alone.

Also: 3 nodules at Khania, 1 at Knossos, 1 at Zakros.

On tablets: co-occurs with **SUS** (pig), **GRA** (grain), **VIN** (wine), **VAS** (vessels).

## The Dual Nature

| Function | Document types | Sites | Count |
|----------|---------------|-------|-------|
| **Logogram** | Nodules, tablets, roundels | HT (36), KH (7) | 36 |
| **Syllabogram** | Stone vessels, scepter, some tablets | 16+ sanctuary sites, ARKH, KN | 11+ |

This dual use is normal in Aegean scripts — the logogram represents a word, and the syllabogram represents the first syllable of that word. So the WORD that *301 stands for as a logogram **begins with the sound** that *301 represents as a syllabogram.

## What Phoneme?

### The constraint
Linear B was adapted from Linear A for Greek. When Greek speakers adapted the script, they dropped signs for sounds their language didn't have. *301 was dropped. Therefore it represents a **phoneme absent from Greek**.

### What Greek lacks

Greek (~1450 BCE) has: p, t, k, kʷ, b, d, g, gʷ, s, h, m, n, l, r, w, j + 5 vowels.

What ancient languages of the region have that Greek doesn't:
- **Pharyngeal fricatives** (ḥ, ʕ) — Semitic, possibly Hurrian
- **Additional uvulars** (beyond the QA/QE series already in LA)
- **Lateral fricatives** (ɬ) — Hattic, some pre-Anatolian substrates
- **Glottalized/ejective stops** (p', t', k') — Caucasian languages
- **Additional sibilants** (š, ts) — Hurrian, Hattic, Luwian

### Manning's 'phra' is unlikely
Manning proposes *301 = CHS #046 ("engraving tool") with value 'phra'. But Greek HAS /phr/ (phren, phrasō). If *301 were /phra/, Linear B would have kept it. This value is driven by the pictogram, not phonology.

### Most likely: a pharyngeal or laryngeal
The most common sound lost when scripts of the eastern Mediterranean are adapted for Greek. If *301 = /ʕa/ or /ħa/:
- a-ta-i-ʕa-wa-ja → a five-syllable ritual term
- The sound would have been unfamiliar to Greek ears and unwritable in their adapted script
- Hurrian has laryngeals; Hattic has pharyngeals; both are candidate relatives

## The Knossos Ivory Scepter (KN Zf 13)

The 19-sign inscription: **A-RE-NE-SI-DI-*301-PI-KE-PA-JA-TA-RI-SE-TE-RI-MU-A-JA-KU**

This is one of the **longest continuous Linear A texts ever found**. *301 appears at position 6, mid-word. If we can read the surrounding signs (which we can, via Linear B values), the phonological context constrains what *301 can be. This scepter inscription could be the key to solving *301.

The recently published (2025) analysis of this scepter from the Knossos cult center describes it as having **119 signs** — the longest known Linear A inscription. If the full publication becomes available, it could transform our understanding of *301.

## What Solving *301 Would Unlock

1. **The libation formula opening** — the most important phrase in Linear A
2. **Several personal names** — zu-*301-se-de-qi, sa-*301-ri, na-tu-*301-ne
3. **The meaning of the most common logogram on Haghia Triada nodules** — what commodity or authorization the 32 sealed objects represent
4. **A core phonological feature of Minoan** — which "exotic" sound the language had
5. **The Knossos scepter inscription** — potentially the longest readable Linear A text
