# Sign *301: The Key to the Lock

## The Most Important Unknown Sign in Linear A

Sign *301 appears on **48 documents** — more frequently than the sign for grain. It has no equivalent in Linear B, meaning it represents a sound that Greek doesn't have. It sits at the exact center of the libation formula, blocking our ability to read the opening phrase. Solve *301, and the oldest prayer in Europe opens up.

**32 nodules at Haghia Triada** — *301 is the ONLY sign stamped on the seal. Whatever commodity or authorization it marks, it was important enough to stand alone.

On tablets: co-occurs with **SUS** (pig), **GRA** (grain), **VIN** (wine), **VAS** (vessels).

## The Dual Nature

| Function | Document types | Sites | Count |
|----------|---------------|-------|-------|
| **Logogram** | Nodules, tablets, roundels | HT (36), KH (7) | 36 |
| **Syllabogram** | Stone vessels, scepter, some tablets | 16+ sanctuary sites, ARKH, KN | 11+ |

This dual use is normal in Aegean scripts — the logogram represents a word, and the syllabogram represents the first syllable of that word. So the WORD that *301 stands for as a logogram **begins with the sound** that *301 represents as a syllabogram.

## What Phoneme?

### The constraint
Linear B was adapted from Linear A for Greek. When Greek speakers adapted the script, they dropped signs for sounds their language didn't have. *301 was dropped. Therefore it represents a **phoneme absent from Greek**.

### What Greek lacks

Greek (~1450 BCE) has: p, t, k, kʷ, b, d, g, gʷ, s, h, m, n, l, r, w, j + 5 vowels.

What ancient languages of the region have that Greek doesn't:
- **Pharyngeal fricatives** (ḥ, ʕ) — Semitic, possibly Hurrian
- **Additional uvulars** (beyond the QA/QE series already in LA)
- **Lateral fricatives** (ɬ) — Hattic, some pre-Anatolian substrates
- **Glottalized/ejective stops** (p', t', k') — Caucasian languages
- **Additional sibilants** (š, ts) — Hurrian, Hattic, Luwian

### Manning's 'phra' is unlikely
Manning proposes *301 = CHS #046 ("engraving tool") with value 'phra'. But Greek HAS /phr/ (phren, phrasō). If *301 were /phra/, Linear B would have kept it. This value is driven by the pictogram, not phonology.

### Most likely: a pharyngeal or laryngeal
The most common sound lost when scripts of the eastern Mediterranean are adapted for Greek. If *301 = /ʕa/ or /ħa/:
- a-ta-i-ʕa-wa-ja → a five-syllable ritual term
- The sound would have been unfamiliar to Greek ears and unwritable in their adapted script
- Hurrian has laryngeals; Hattic has pharyngeals; both are candidate relatives

## The Knossos Ivory Scepter (KN Zf 13)

The 19-sign inscription: **A-RE-NE-SI-DI-*301-PI-KE-PA-JA-TA-RI-SE-TE-RI-MU-A-JA-KU**

This is one of the **longest continuous Linear A texts ever found**. *301 appears at position 6, mid-word. If we can read the surrounding signs (which we can, via Linear B values), the phonological context constrains what *301 can be. This scepter inscription could be the key to solving *301.

The recently published (2025) analysis of this scepter from the Knossos cult center describes it as having **119 signs** — the longest known Linear A inscription. If the full publication becomes available, it could transform our understanding of *301.

## What Solving *301 Would Unlock

1. **The libation formula opening** — the most important phrase in Linear A
2. **Several personal names** — zu-*301-se-de-qi, sa-*301-ri, na-tu-*301-ne
3. **The meaning of the most common logogram on Haghia Triada nodules** — what commodity or authorization the 32 sealed objects represent
4. **A core phonological feature of Minoan** — which "exotic" sound the language had
5. **The Knossos scepter inscription** — potentially the longest readable Linear A text

## Open Questions

- Does *301 appear on the Arkhanes seal stones (~2000 BCE) in its Cretan Hieroglyphic form (#046)?
- Is the **doubled logogram** *301-*301 (HT 26b.4) a plural marker, an intensifier, or a different concept?
- The **I+*301 heading ligature** on tablets — is this an institution, a person, or a commodity class?
- Can distributional analysis narrow the phoneme? (What sounds appear before and after *301 in attested words?)
- The Knossos scepter (119 signs!) — when the full text is published, does it contain *301 multiple times?

Combining both notations: **31 distinct words** containing *301 across the corpus. The earlier "15+ word contexts" and "14 words" figures both undercounted. The scepter's *301 occurrence is captured (position 6 of a 19-sign word); the three words cited in the body as missing from the data layer (`zu-*301-se-de-qi-*118`, `sa-*301-ri`, `te-*301`) are all present, just stored under `A301` rather than `*301`.

The "canonical" libation formula is attested **10 times** as `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja`, at the following sanctuaries: Ioukhtas (3×), Kophinas, Palaikastro, Syme (3×), Troullos (Archanes sanctuary), Vrysinas. Four further variants — `a-ta-i-*301-wa-e`, `a-ta-i-*301-de-ka`, `a-na-ti-*301-wa-ja`, `ta-na-i-*301-u-ti-nu` — each have a single attestation. Adding `ja-ta-i-*301-u-ja` (AP Za 1) gives **6 formula-shaped words with *301 at position 4** across 15 documents, plus the two outliers `a-*301-ki-ta-a` and `na-tu-*301-ne` that share the syllabogram in non-final position.

So the "7 words + regional variants" framing the audit asked for resolves to:

1. **Core formula** — `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja` (10 attestations, 7 sites)
2. **Variant with -e ending** — `a-ta-i-*301-wa-e` (Palaikastro)
3. **Variant with -de-ka ending** — `a-ta-i-*301-de-ka` (Zakros)
4. **Variant with JA- prefix** — `ja-ta-i-*301-u-ja` (Apodoulou)
5. **Variant with A-NA- prefix** — `a-na-ti-*301-wa-ja` (Ioukhtas)
6. **TA-NA extended variant** — `ta-na-i-*301-u-ti-nu` (Ioukhtas)
7. **Outlier with *301 non-final** — `a-*301-ki-ta-a` (Tylissos)

`na-tu-*301-ne` (Skoteino Zc 1) sits on the boundary between "formula variant" and "independent word"; treat as an open item.

### GORILA sign index cross-check (completed)

The newly extracted GORILA Volume 5 sign index records **35 rows** for A 301, distributed across all rubriques (alone, as-first, as-non-initial, as-last, composite catalog, headers). Cross-checking the 72 corpus documents against those 35 index rows yields the following:

**Where corpus agrees with GORILA's sign index (syllabogram uses in running text):**  After normalization for sub-face suffixes (corpus `IO Za 1` ↔ index `IO Za 1a`, corpus `ZA Zb 3` ↔ index `ZA Za 3`, etc.), 6 of 7 apparent mismatches resolve cleanly. The one genuine absence is **`NO Za 1a` (Nerokourou)** — GORILA's sign index cites it for A 301, but no `NO Za 1` row exists in `corpus.json`. This is a coverage gap and should be ingested.

**Where corpus has *301 but GORILA's sign index does not cite the document:** 52 corpus documents. Of these, **45 are HT Wa entries** — Haghia Triada roundel and nodule sealings. Their absence from the sign index is expected behaviour: GORILA catalogues sealings separately (Volume 2), and the Volume 5 sign index is a syllabogram-in-word concordance. The HT Wa materials use A 301 as a standalone logogram (one sign per sealing), which is indexed in the sealings volume rather than here. No action needed; the project data layer has both and knows how to distinguish them.

The remaining 7 non-HT-Wa absences should be hand-verified — they may be recent additions, sub-entries GORILA didn't separately list, or documents GORILA handles in a different rubric. See `linear_a/data/sign_301_cross_check.json` for the full per-document comparison.
