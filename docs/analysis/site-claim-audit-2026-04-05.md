## Revised `gorila-301-findings.md`

## Verdict
**Substantially improved and now broadly trustworthy as a research note.**

### What improved
- no longer confuses distinct *301-containing contexts with “regional variants” of the same formula word
- clearly states the breakthrough is **distributional / primary-source**, not decipherment
- separates:
  - canonical formula confirmation
  - additional *301 word contexts
  - Khania administrative compounds/ligatures
  - open audit items
- clearly flags what is not yet safe to claim publicly

### Safe claims from the document
- GORILA Vol 5 sign-index extraction materially improves the *301 context picture
- `a-ta-i-*301-wa-ja` is confirmed at stronger primary-source level
- *301 appears in a broader range of contexts than the earlier smaller-sample verifier pass captured
- Khania roundels show productive *301 compounding worth further study

### Not yet safe
- a final exact count of distinct *301 words
- strong regional-variant claims without document-level re-reading
- phonetic or semantic value assignment

### Recommended next step
The new file is good enough to use internally and cautiously publicly, but it should still be paired with a dedicated verifier follow-up:
- `docs/analysis/verifier-gorila-301-audit.md`

## Homepage claim audit (`index.html`)

The homepage is still the main location where the project outruns its best evidence.

## Priority fixes

### 1. “Reading the oldest prayer in Europe”
**Current risk:** too definitive

**Better:**
- “Toward reading the oldest prayer in Europe”
- “A partial reading of Europe’s oldest known prayer formula”
- “The oldest prayer in Europe may be partially readable”

### 2. `ja-sa-sa-ra-me` “is almost certainly Luwian ...”
**Current risk:** too strong for homepage voice

**Better:**
- “is best explained as Luwian ...”
- “strongly suggests Luwian ...”

### 3. `i-da-ma-te` = “Mother of Ida”
**Current risk:** too flatly translated

**Better:**
- “best current interpretation: ‘Mother of Ida’”
- “likely invokes Ida and a maternal/divine title”

### 4. “It represents a sound that Greek doesn’t have”
**Current risk:** too phonologically final

**Better:**
- “may represent a value not preserved in Linear B Greek”
- “likely marks something not straightforwardly mapped into Greek orthography”

### 5. “not Greek, not Semitic”
**Current risk:** too absolute without qualifier

**Better:**
- “current evidence argues strongly against Greek or Semitic as the core language”
- “the morphology is difficult to reconcile with Greek or Semitic”

### 6. “agglutinative language isolate”
**Current risk:** isolate remains too strong

**Better:**
- “a probably agglutinative language of still-unknown affiliation”
- “an unidentified suffixing/agglutinative language”

### 7. “labyrinth is a Minoan word”
**Current risk:** hypothesis presented as settled fact

**Better:**
- “may underlie Greek labyrinthos”
- “is a leading proposed ancestor of Greek labyrinthos”

### 8. “Writing began with prayer, not bookkeeping”
**Current risk:** beautiful but too strong

**Better:**
- “writing in Europe may have begun with ritual use, not just bookkeeping”
- “some of the earliest Cretan writing may already have had a religious function”

### Strong and keep
- the project origin story
- corpus-unification explanation
- Builder / Verifier collaboration explanation
- GORILA acquisition saga
- “not a decipherment” framing

### Needs softening

#### “Greek is not the language of Linear A.”
Better:
- “Greek is currently a weak fit for the language of Linear A”
- “the morphology makes Greek difficult to sustain as the core-language explanation”

#### `ja-sa-sa-ra-me` “is almost certainly Luwian”
Better:
- “is best explained as Luwian”

#### “labyrinth is a Minoan word”
Better:
- “labyrinth may preserve a Minoan/Anatolian-linked lexical inheritance”
- or a more cautious derivational note

#### “most complete contextual distribution ever assembled”
This should be scoped.

Better:
- “most complete open digital contextual distribution currently assembled in the project”
- “most complete open digital *301 context inventory we have yet assembled”

## Bottom line

The project site is better than it was yesterday.

The revised *301 findings note is a real improvement and no longer suffers from the same overclaiming problem as the first draft. The About page is strong. The discovery story is good and useful.

The main remaining weakness is not lack of substance. It is **sentence-level truth discipline on the homepage and a few narrative pages**. The site is strongest when it says:
- here is the data
- here is our best interpretation
- here is how sure we are

It is weakest when it says:
- here is the most dramatic phrasing of the story as if it were already settled fact

That is a very fixable problem.
