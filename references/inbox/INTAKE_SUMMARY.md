## What This Changes About Our Project

### Confirmed strengths:
- Our corpus (1,880 docs) is **larger than any single published reference** cites (~1,500 per Salgarella/Steele)
- Our SigLA integration aligns with the leading scholar's own work
- Our glossary entries for *ku-ro* and *ki-ro* are well-supported across multiple sources

### New things to incorporate:
1. **Younger's Lexicon** — the most complete word list available. Should cross-reference with our glossary.
2. **Younger's Updates** — contains corrections to specific tablet readings we may have wrong.
3. **Salgarella's sign classification** — her 2022 re-classification paper needs to be acquired (only front matter in our PDF).
4. **Van Soesbergen's Vol II transliterated corpus** — useful as a structured data source regardless of the Hurrian hypothesis.
5. **Schurr's ki.ki.ra.ja analysis** — expand our glossary entry for ki.ro derivatives.
6. **Melena's Linear B reference** — essential for all LA↔LB sign value cross-referencing.

### Decipherment landscape:
- **Hurrian hypothesis** (van Soesbergen): most developed, 6+ volumes, minority but serious
- **Greek hypothesis** (Mosenkis): multiple papers, not mainstream
- **Luwian hypothesis** (Palmer/Georgiev): historical, largely abandoned
- **Sumerian hypothesis** (Kenanidis/Papakitsos): fringe
- **No consensus**: the language remains unidentified. Our project's stance (confidence scores, evidence chains, no commitment to a language family) is correct.
